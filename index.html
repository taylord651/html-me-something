My Tech Journey

By Domonique Taylor

Global Hackathon

What was I thinking? Actually, I thought a lot about the fact that I had no coding experince and doubted how much I would be able to contribute to the team project. I was also nervous about finding a team, considering that I had little to add in regards to coding our project.

I had a great time and enjoyed meeting new people. My team was really cool about me not knowing any coding and even tried to teach me. The terms Github, Django, Python, DevPost and more were thrown around a lot. Now that I am taking LC101, they sound familiar

AfroTech

In November, 2018 I traveled to San Francisco to attend the AfroTech conference. Thousands of black professionals in tech career fields gathered to learn about entreprenuership, technology and business. Although I didn't learn the technical skills needed to start a career in tech, I met many people who were willing to share advice.

St. Louis Public Library

I took an online "Intro to Java" class through Gale Courses via the St. Louis Public Library. I completed the course, but was no where near being proficient in the coding language. I learned how to print "Hello World" (System.out.println("Hello World!");) in BlueJ, and Integrated Development Environment (IDE)

LaunchCode

I applied for a LaunchCode class three times. First, I applied in the summer of 2016. I never completed the pre-work, and therefore, was not able to join the class. I applied again in December of 2018 after completing the "Intro to Java" course. I wanted to learn more. Third time is a charm, and I applied again in March of 2019. The pre-work/quiz questions were identical to the questions from my December application, so I felt confident in this part.
